Current Work

How Effective is Hamburg's Solar Roof Mandate?

Bachelor's Thesis · University of Hamburg · 2026 · Supervisor: Prof. Lange

Hamburg is unique among German states in mandating solar panels on private residential buildings — a policy that took effect for new constructions in January 2023 and extended to roof renovations in January 2024. I use a staggered difference-in-differences design, comparing Hamburg's solar adoption against six surrounding Landkreise in Schleswig-Holstein and Niedersachsen, to estimate the causal effect of the mandate on residential PV uptake.

The analysis draws on the German plant register (Marktstammdatenregister), building completion data, and operator-type breakdowns to separate private from commercial installations. A key empirical challenge is disentangling the mandate's effect from the nationwide solar boom triggered by the 2023 EEG reform.
